Burj khalifa
Burj Khalifa: Let’s cut to the chase—you haven’t really seen Dubai until you’ve stood at the base of the Burj Khalifa, craning your neck until it hurts. This isn’t just a tall building; it’s a middle finger to gravity. At 828 meters, the views from "At the Top" (124th floor) will make your stomach drop—the city sprawls beneath you like a toy model, with tiny cars inching along highways and the desert bleeding into the ocean. Go at sunset when the light turns the glass towers into molten gold. Pro tip: Skip the basic tickets and splurge on the SKY level (148th floor)—you’ll bypass the crowds and get a champagne toast that feels ridiculously fancy when you’re hovering a half-mile in the air.
